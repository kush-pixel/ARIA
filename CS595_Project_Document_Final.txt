2. INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

2a. Problem Description

A general practitioner managing roughly 1,800 hypertensive patients gets about
8 minutes per consultation. When a patient walks in, the GP typically has access
to a single clinic blood pressure reading from three months ago, a static
medication list from the last visit, and no structured record of what happened
in between. Home readings, missed doses, worsening trends, and drug interactions
introduced after the last visit are all invisible until the patient arrives.

This gap creates two compounding problems in hypertension management.

The first is therapeutic inertia. Estimates suggest it contributes to 40 to 80
percent of uncontrolled hypertension cases. The GP sees persistently elevated
blood pressure but makes no medication adjustment - not because they are unaware
of the problem in general, but because they lack structured evidence that the
current regimen is failing for this specific patient at this specific point in
time. Without a clear picture of the home reading trend and whether medications
have been taken consistently, the GP has limited grounds to act.

The second is the inability to distinguish between two clinically different
patients who look identical on paper. Patient A has poorly controlled blood
pressure because they are not taking their medications reliably. Patient B has
poorly controlled blood pressure despite taking their medications consistently.
These two situations call for completely different responses. The first suggests
a behavioral conversation about adherence barriers. The second indicates that
the treatment itself may need to be reconsidered. Without data connecting
adherence behavior to blood pressure trajectory, the GP cannot tell which
patient they are dealing with.

There is also a drug interaction blind spot. A patient who added an NSAID such
as Voltaren for knee pain since their last hypertension review may now be in a
triple whammy drug interaction with their ACE inhibitor and diuretic. Without
a structured check at each encounter, this combination can persist unnoticed
across visits.

ARIA addresses all three of these problems by generating a structured pre-visit
clinical intelligence briefing at 7:30 AM on appointment day - before the
patient walks in. The GP receives a prioritized summary of what happened between
visits, with drug interaction flags, adherence signals, blood pressure trend
analysis, and a structured visit agenda - all derived from home monitoring data
and the patient's EHR record.


2b. Purpose

ARIA is a between-visit clinical intelligence platform for hypertension
management at general practice scale. It does four things:

1. Ingests structured patient EHR data via FHIR R4 Bundle from the clinic's
   iEMR system.

2. Monitors home blood pressure and medication adherence via the CuffLink
   patient app.

3. Analyzes this data through a three-layer AI pipeline - deterministic rules,
   weighted risk scoring, and LLM narrative - plus a deterministic drug
   interaction detector.

4. Delivers a prioritized pre-visit clinical briefing, sorted by patient
   urgency, so the GP knows exactly what to discuss in those 8 minutes.

Goals and objectives:

- Detect reading gaps before they become clinical gaps. A High Risk patient
  with CHF who stops measuring at home is flagged after a single missed day.

- Identify therapeutic inertia. Flag patients whose blood pressure has remained
  persistently elevated above their adaptive personal threshold while their
  medication regimen has been unchanged for the duration of the window.

- Surface adherence concerns. Distinguish patients with poor adherence from
  patients who are adherent but not responding to treatment. These two groups
  require different clinical conversations.

- Detect deterioration. Identify patients whose blood pressure is trending
  upward, including sudden step-change rises, not just gradual slopes.

- Check drug interactions. Run four deterministic rules against the current
  medication list at every briefing generation, with severity escalation when
  relevant comorbidities are present.

- Score and prioritize patients. Compute a weighted numeric risk score
  (0.0-100.0) for every monitoring-active patient and sort the dashboard by
  tier first, then score within each tier.

- Generate a readable briefing. Convert the deterministic briefing payload into
  a 3-sentence clinician-readable summary using an LLM with 11 validation
  checks before any output reaches the UI.

- Validate against physician clinical judgment. Run shadow mode evaluation
  against the physician's own historical HTN concern assessments from the iEMR
  dataset to measure how well ARIA's alert logic aligns with clinical ground
  truth.


2c. Scope

Included in Scope

Disease Focus: Hypertension as the sole disease module. The system is
architected to support additional disease modules, but no other disease is
implemented.

EHR Ingestion: FHIR R4 Bundle import from the iEMR dataset. The adapter
converts iEMR JSON to FHIR resources. The ingestion layer only sees FHIR
resources - it has no awareness of the source EHR format.

Three-Layer AI Pipeline:
  - Layer 1: Five deterministic detectors - gap detection, therapeutic inertia,
    adherence-BP correlation, deterioration, and variability detection.
  - Layer 2: Weighted risk scoring using five clinical signals (systolic vs
    personal baseline, medication inertia, inverted adherence, reading gap, and
    comorbidity severity). Output is a 0.0-100.0 score stored on the patients
    table.
  - Layer 3: LLM narrative generation. A 3-sentence readable summary generated
    after the deterministic briefing is complete and validated, with 11
    checks before any output is stored.

Drug Interaction Detection: Four deterministic rules run at every briefing
generation. No LLM is involved. Severity escalates based on active
comorbidities.

Risk Tier System: Three-tier classification (High/Medium/Low) with immovable
ingestion-time overrides for CHF, Stroke, and TIA, nightly algorithmic
reclassification with hysteresis, and a clinician manual override endpoint.

Clinician Dashboard: Patient list sorted by tier then risk score, individual
patient briefing pages, alert inbox with escalation and calibration suppression,
and a clinical chatbot embedded in the briefing page.

Patient PWA (CuffLink): An installable Progressive Web App for home BP
submission and medication confirmation. JWT authentication with a separate
patient secret from the clinician secret.

Synthetic Data Engine: Generates clinically realistic home BP readings and
medication confirmation records interpolated from clinic anchor points with
peer-reviewed variance parameters.

Shadow Mode Validation: Offline evaluation that replays the alert engine at
each historical clinic visit using only data available before that visit, then
compares ARIA's output against the physician's recorded HTN concern assessment
from the iEMR data.

Audit Logging: Immutable audit_events table capturing bundle imports, reading
ingestion, briefing views, alert acknowledgments, LLM validation attempts, and
tier reclassification events.

Background Processing: APScheduler-driven 7:30 AM briefing generation for
patients with appointments, midnight pattern recompute sweep for all
monitoring-active patients, and a 30-second poll-based job worker.


Excluded from Scope

- OpenMRS integration (identified as a stretch goal in the original project
  plan; not implemented)
- BLE Bluetooth device integration (GATT 0x1810 - infrastructure partially
  present as a webhook endpoint, but no physical device pairing)
- Real-time pharmacy data feeds or electronic prescription access
- Wearable, CGM, or IoT device integration
- Machine learning models for prediction or adaptive threshold learning
- Type 2 Diabetes extension module
- Personalized per-patient baseline modeling using ML
- Full production EHR integration beyond the iEMR prototype environment
- HIPAA-compliant production hardening or enterprise security infrastructure
- Autonomous clinical decision-making
- Multi-patient shadow mode validation (currently validated on patient 1091 only)
- Live physician-AI comparison UI showing match/partial match/mismatch alongside
  iEMR physician judgments (validation runs offline via shadow mode script)
- Structured clinician agreement capture panel in the dashboard UI (individual
  alert dispositions are supported; the three-input structured comparison panel
  from the original proposal was not built)


2d. Audience

Primary Users - Clinicians (Physicians, Nurse Practitioners, Cardiologists,
Internists, Care Managers)

These are the users ARIA is built for. They manage large hypertension panels
under time pressure - roughly 8 minutes per consultation - with limited
visibility into what happened between appointments. They are comfortable with
clinical summaries but appropriately skeptical of unexplained AI outputs.
They need to see what data drove each finding, not just a risk score. They
also need a fast way to override the system when they have clinical context
that ARIA does not.

Secondary Users - Population Health Managers and Care Coordinators

Users who monitor patient populations at the panel level rather than the
individual patient level. They care about which patient segments are most at
risk and where attention is needed at scale. The tier-filtered dashboard view
and alert inbox serve this audience.

Tertiary Users - Health Informatics Researchers and Students

The prototype demonstrates a complete AI decision support pipeline including
FHIR-native EHR ingestion, multi-signal deterministic analysis, constrained
LLM generation, and physician-validated shadow mode evaluation against real
clinical data. This audience evaluates the system for technical rigor and
clinical validity methodology.


2e. Overview

ARIA is a clinical intelligence platform that sits between EHR visits. It
consists of five integrated components:

1. EHR Integration - The iEMR adapter converts raw iEMR JSON to FHIR R4
   Bundle format. The ingestion layer validates the bundle, populates 12
   database tables, and triggers ingestion-time risk tier overrides for
   patients with CHF, Stroke, or TIA in their problem list.

2. Synthetic Data Engine - Generates home BP readings and medication
   confirmations across the patient's full care history by interpolating
   between clinic anchor points. Day-to-day systolic SD is 8-12 mmHg,
   morning readings are 5-10 mmHg higher than evening, device outage periods
   appear as absent rows, and a white-coat dip appears in the 3-5 days before
   each appointment.

3. Three-Layer AI Pipeline - Layer 1 runs five deterministic detectors and
   fires alerts. Layer 2 computes a weighted numeric risk score. Layer 3
   converts the deterministic briefing JSON into a 3-sentence readable summary
   after 11 validation checks pass.

4. Clinician Dashboard - Patient list sorted by risk urgency, individual
   briefing pages with drug interactions and visit agenda, alert inbox with
   escalation and calibration suppression, and an embedded clinical chatbot
   (Ask ARIA).

5. Patient PWA (CuffLink) - A Progressive Web App at port 3001 where
   patients submit home BP readings and confirm medication doses. Patients
   never see their own clinical interpretation, risk tier, or risk score.

All clinical outputs are framed as decision support only. The system enforces
clinical language constraints at both the deterministic layer and the LLM
validation layer. "Possible adherence concern" is used instead of
"non-adherent." "Treatment review warranted" is used instead of "medication
failure." No dose-change recommendations are ever generated.


2f. Glossary / Terminology

FHIR R4 - HL7 Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources version 4. The
standard format used to represent and exchange healthcare data. ARIA uses FHIR
R4 Bundle resources as the interchange format between the iEMR adapter and the
ingestion layer.

iEMR - The de-identified electronic medical record dataset provided by
Leap of Faith Technologies. Contains real longitudinal clinical data from
actual patient visits, including BP readings, medication history, encounter
dates, and physician-recorded HTN concern assessments per visit.

Therapeutic Inertia - The failure to intensify or adjust treatment when a
patient's blood pressure remains persistently above their clinical target
despite an unchanged medication regimen. Estimated to contribute to 40-80
percent of uncontrolled hypertension cases.

Layer 1 / Layer 2 / Layer 3 - The three sequential stages of ARIA's AI
pipeline. Layer 1 is deterministic rule-based detection. Layer 2 is weighted
numeric risk scoring. Layer 3 is LLM narrative generation. Layers run in
strict order; Layer 3 never runs before Layer 1 is complete.

Risk Tier - The categorical patient classification: High, Medium, or Low.
Controls primary sort order on the dashboard. Set at ingestion for patients
with CHF, Stroke, or TIA (immovable floor); otherwise set algorithmically by
the nightly reclassification job or manually by the clinician.

Risk Score - A numeric priority score from 0.0 to 100.0 computed by Layer 2.
Controls sort order within each tier. Computed from five weighted signals:
systolic vs personal baseline (30%), medication inertia (25%), inverted
adherence (20%), reading gap (15%), comorbidity severity (10%).

Adaptive Window - The detection window used by all Layer 1 detectors and Layer
2 scoring. Calculated as the interval between the patient's last visit date and
next appointment, bounded between 14 and 90 days. Falls back to 28 days when
appointment data is unavailable.

Patient-Adaptive Threshold - The systolic blood pressure threshold used by the
inertia and deterioration detectors for each individual patient. Calculated as
max(130, stable_baseline_mean + 1.5 * SD) capped at 145 mmHg. Adjusted down
7 mmHg for patients with CHF, Stroke, or TIA.

White-Coat Dip - The observed drop in home BP readings in the days before a
clinical appointment. The detectors exclude readings within 5 days of the next
appointment to prevent this dip from masking legitimate inertia or
deterioration flags.

Drug Interaction Detector - A deterministic rule checker that runs four
interaction rules against the patient's current medication list at every
briefing generation. No LLM is involved. Four rules: NSAID + antihypertensive,
triple whammy (NSAID + ACE/ARB + diuretic), K-sparing diuretic + ACE/ARB, and
beta-blocker + non-DHP calcium channel blocker.

Shadow Mode - The offline validation system that replays ARIA's alert engine at
each historical clinic visit using only data available before that visit, then
compares the output against the physician's recorded HTN concern assessment
(PROBLEM_STATUS2_FLAG: 3=stable, 2=concerned, 1=urgent).

CuffLink - The patient-facing app and brand name for ARIA's home monitoring
layer. A Progressive Web App (Next.js 14) installable on iOS, Android, and
desktop.

Cold-Start Suppression - When a patient has been enrolled for fewer than 21
days, the inertia, deterioration, and adherence detectors are suppressed. The
gap detector still runs. This prevents false positives from insufficient data.

3. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

3a. Architecture

ARIA follows a layered architecture with a clear separation between data
ingestion, analysis, presentation, and patient monitoring.

EHR Ingestion Layer
  iEMR JSON --> Adapter (adapter.py) --> FHIR R4 Bundle
  --> Ingestion (ingestion.py) --> 12 PostgreSQL tables
  --> Ingestion-time auto-overrides (CHF/Stroke/TIA --> High Risk)

Analysis Pipeline (Background Worker)
  Queued job (processing_jobs) --> 30-second poll worker
  --> Layer 1: 5 deterministic detectors (gap, inertia, adherence,
      deterioration, variability)
  --> Layer 2: Weighted risk score (0.0-100.0) stored on patients table
  --> Nightly tier reclassification with hysteresis

Briefing Generation (APScheduler at 7:30 AM)
  Briefing job queued for appointment-day patients
  --> Drug interaction check (4 rules, deterministic)
  --> Briefing payload composed and stored
  --> Layer 3: LLM summary generated and validated (11 checks)
  --> Briefing written to database

Clinician Dashboard (Next.js 14, port 3000)
  GET /api/patients --> patients sorted by tier, then risk score
  GET /api/briefings/{id} --> most-recent active briefing
  GET /api/alerts --> active unacknowledged alerts
  POST /api/chat --> Ask ARIA chatbot

Patient PWA (Next.js 14, port 3001)
  POST /api/readings --> home BP submission
  POST /api/confirmations/confirm --> medication confirmation
  GET /api/confirmations/pending --> upcoming doses

Backend: FastAPI at port 8000. All routes are async. No operations run in
the HTTP request path that are not required for that request.

Database: PostgreSQL via Supabase. 12 tables with a full index set including
a unique idempotency index on processing_jobs, a unique reading deduplication
index (patient, datetime, source), and a unique confirmation deduplication index
(patient, medication, scheduled time).

Primary Data - iEMR Dataset (Leap of Faith Technologies / Dr. Rad)
  De-identified longitudinal clinical records from real patient visits.
  Demo patient 1091: 124 clinic visits over 11 years (2002-2015), 65 in-clinic
  BP readings (mean 133.8 mmHg, SD 16.2 mmHg), 14 active medications including
  a triple whammy interaction, and 17 coded problems (CHF I50.9, T2DM E11,
  CAD I25, HTN I10, and others).

Synthetic Home Reading Data
  Generated by reading_generator.py for each patient. Interpolates linearly
  between consecutive clinic BP anchor points with Gaussian noise. Day-to-day
  systolic SD is 8-12 mmHg (never flat). For patient 1091, the generator
  produces 1,800+ home readings across the 2008-2015 period. Medication
  confirmations are generated separately using a Beta distribution
  (alpha=6.5, beta=0.65, mean ~91%) per medication per inter-visit interval.

Demo Patient Set (4 patients)
  Patient A (1091) - Therapeutic inertia + triple whammy drug interaction.
    CHF auto-override. High Risk.
  Patient B (DEMO_EHR) - EHR-only patient, no home readings. Overdue labs.
    NSAID + antihypertensive interaction. monitoring_active=False.
  Patient C (DEMO_GAP) - 82 days of readings, then a 9-12 day gap.
    Gap alert and inertia alert expected.
  Patient D (DEMO_ADH) - 28 days of readings, ~58% adherence rate.
    Adherence Pattern A alert expected.

Data accessibility: The iEMR dataset is provided by Leap of Faith Technologies
and is stored locally in data/raw/iemr/. Synthetic data is generated on demand
from the clinic anchor points in the database. All demo data is reproducible
by running python scripts/setup_demo.py from the project root.

4b. User Stories

As a clinician opening my morning dashboard, I want to see which of my
patients most urgently need attention today, so I can prioritize my
consultations before anyone walks in.

As a clinician reviewing a patient briefing, I want to see drug interaction
flags with plain-English descriptions and severity levels, so I can decide
whether to review the medication list during the visit.

As a clinician, I want to see whether a patient's elevated blood pressure is
associated with low adherence or high adherence, so I know whether to have a
medication conversation or an adherence conversation.

As a clinician, I want to override a patient's risk tier with a documented
reason, and have that decision protected for a defined review period, so the
algorithm does not reverse my clinical judgment the next morning.

As a clinician, I want to ask the system a natural-language question about a
patient (such as why their score is high or what their adherence was last
month) without having to navigate through multiple screens.

As a clinician, I want the visit agenda to tell me the 3-6 most important
things to discuss with this patient, ordered by clinical priority, so those
8 minutes have structure.

As a patient, I want to submit my morning and evening blood pressure readings
quickly from my phone, so my GP has an accurate picture of what my blood
pressure has been doing between appointments.

As a patient, I want to confirm when I have taken my medications so that my
GP can see my adherence record without me having to explain it at every visit.


4c. Use Cases

Use Case 1: Clinician Morning Dashboard Review
  Actor: Clinician
  Pre-condition: At least one monitoring-active patient with a briefing
  generated for today's appointment.
  Flow: Clinician opens dashboard, reviews sorted patient list, filters by
  High Risk if needed, opens a briefing, reads AI summary, reviews drug
  interactions and visit agenda, acknowledges relevant alerts, closes briefing.
  Post-condition: Briefing read_at is set. Audit event logged.

Use Case 2: Clinician Alert Acknowledgment
  Actor: Clinician
  Pre-condition: Active unacknowledged alert visible in alert inbox.
  Flow: Clinician reviews alert, clicks acknowledge. System sets
  acknowledged_at. Audit event logged. Alert disappears from inbox.
  Clinician can undo within 24 hours.
  Post-condition: Alert acknowledged, audit event written.

Use Case 3: Clinician Risk Tier Override
  Actor: Clinician
  Pre-condition: Patient does not have a system-override tier (CHF/Stroke/TIA).
  Flow: Clinician submits PATCH /api/patients/{id}/tier with new tier and
  documented reason. System validates. If demotion, sets
  tier_override_suppressed_until = now + 28 days.
  Post-condition: Tier updated, suppression set. Nightly job will not reverse
  for 28 days unless risk score >= 85.
  Exception: Returns 409 if patient has a system-override tier (EHR update
  and re-ingestion required to change these patients).

Use Case 4: Patient BP Submission
  Actor: Patient (authenticated)
  Pre-condition: Patient logged into CuffLink PWA with valid JWT.
  Flow: Patient enters two readings, selects session type, optionally records
  symptoms and whether medication was taken. Submits form.
  System validates reading plausibility. Stores to readings table. Writes
  audit event (reading_ingested).
  Post-condition: Reading stored, audit logged, background worker picks up
  and runs Layer 1 on next sweep.

Use Case 5: Clinician Chatbot Query (Ask ARIA)
  Actor: Clinician
  Pre-condition: Clinician has opened a patient briefing page.
  Flow: Clinician types a natural-language question. Chatbot calls one or
  more tool functions (get_briefing, get_readings, get_alerts). Responds with
  a direct answer grounded in patient data. Three-layer guardrails prevent
  off-topic responses, cross-patient queries, and prescriptive language.
  Post-condition: Clinician receives a data-grounded answer.

4e. List of Functional Requirements

FR-01: The system shall ingest patient EHR data via FHIR R4 Bundle format.
  The adapter shall convert iEMR JSON to FHIR resources without the ingestion
  layer having direct knowledge of iEMR field names.

FR-02: The system shall apply immovable High Risk overrides at ingestion for
  patients with CHF (I50), Haemorrhagic Stroke (I61), Ischaemic Stroke (I63/
  I64), or TIA (G45) in the problem list. These overrides cannot be reversed
  via the clinician tier endpoint.

FR-03: Layer 1 shall run five deterministic detectors for every
  monitoring-active patient on the nightly sweep: gap detection, therapeutic
  inertia, adherence-BP correlation, deterioration, and variability.

FR-04: The gap detector shall flag based on tier-appropriate thresholds.
  High Risk: flag at 1 day, urgent at 3 days. Medium: flag at 3 days, urgent
  at 5 days. Low: flag at 7 days, urgent at 14 days.

FR-05: The therapeutic inertia detector shall use a patient-adaptive threshold
  derived from stable historic readings, not a population constant. Comorbidity
  adjustment (-7 mmHg for CHF/Stroke/TIA, floor 130) shall be applied.
  White-coat exclusion (readings within 5 days of next appointment) shall be
  applied before threshold comparison.

FR-06: The adherence-BP correlation detector shall classify each patient into
  Pattern A (high BP + low adherence), Pattern B (high BP + high adherence),
  or Pattern C (normal BP + low adherence). Pattern B shall be suppressed
  during active titration windows (drug-class-aware: diuretics/beta-blockers
  14 days, ACE/ARBs 28 days, amlodipine 56 days) when BP is actively
  declining.

FR-07: The deterioration detector shall require three gates: positive linear
  slope, recent 3-day average above days 4-10 baseline, and recent average at
  or above patient-adaptive threshold. A step-change sub-detector shall fire
  independently when the 7-day recent rolling mean exceeds the 7-day mean from
  three weeks ago by 15 mmHg or more.

FR-08: Layer 2 shall compute a weighted risk score using five signals. Weights:
  systolic vs personal baseline 30%, medication inertia 25%, inverted adherence
  20%, reading gap 15%, comorbidity severity 10%. Comorbidity severity shall
  use a points-based severity-weighted scheme, not a raw count.

FR-09: The risk score and risk_score_computed_at shall be stored on the
  patients table after every Layer 2 computation. A staleness badge shall
  appear in the dashboard when risk_score_computed_at is more than 26 hours old.

FR-10: The drug interaction checker shall evaluate four rules: NSAID +
  antihypertensive (warning), triple whammy (concern, escalates to critical
  when CHF + CKD present), K-sparing diuretic + ACE/ARB (warning, escalates
  when CKD), beta-blocker + non-DHP CCB (concern). Triple whammy shall
  supersede the simpler NSAID rule when both apply.

FR-11: Layer 3 shall generate a 3-sentence readable summary from the
  deterministic briefing payload. Eleven validation checks shall pass before
  the summary is stored. If validation fails after one retry, readable_summary
  shall be stored as null. Every validation attempt shall write an audit_events
  row.

FR-12: Layer 3 shall never produce output containing: "non-adherent",
  "non-compliant", "hypertensive crisis", "medication failure", dose-change
  instructions, the word "prescribe", the word "diagnose", "emergency", "tell
  the patient", patient ID verbatim, or prompt injection markers.

FR-13: GET /api/briefings/{patient_id} shall return only the most-recent
  active briefing (appointment_date >= today OR appointment_date IS NULL).
  Past appointment briefings shall not be returned.

FR-14: The clinician dashboard patient list shall sort by risk tier (High >
  Medium > Low) first, then by risk score descending within each tier.

FR-15: The BP Trend value shown in the dashboard patient list shall use the
  trend_avg_systolic field from the active briefing as the single source of
  truth. Independent recomputation in the frontend shall only occur when no
  active briefing exists.

FR-16: Patients shall never see their own BP readings, risk tier, risk score,
  or any clinical interpretation via the patient app.

FR-17: Every bundle import, reading ingestion, briefing view, alert
  acknowledgment, LLM validation attempt, and tier reclassification shall
  produce an audit_events row with actor_type, actor_id, patient_id, action,
  resource_type, resource_id, and outcome (success or failure).

FR-18: The nightly tier reclassification shall apply a hysteresis band between
  the promotion threshold (score >= 75 for medium to high) and the demotion
  threshold (score < 40 for high to medium). Transitions shall guard against
  oscillation without requiring a stored history of previous scores.

FR-19: The clinician tier override endpoint shall set a 28-day suppression
  window on demotion (aligned to NICE NG136 4-week review standard). Score >=
  85 during suppression shall break the suppression and allow promotion. System-
  override patients (CHF/Stroke/TIA) shall return 409.

FR-20: The Ask ARIA chatbot shall apply three-layer guardrails: off-topic
  detection, scope check (no cross-patient queries), and output validation
  (no prescriptive language or dose instructions).

FR-21: Synthetic home readings shall have day-to-day systolic SD of 8-12 mmHg,
  morning readings 5-10 mmHg above evening, device outage periods as absent
  rows (never null values), and a white-coat dip of 10-15 mmHg in the 3-5 days
  before each appointment.

FR-22: Cold-start suppression shall prevent inertia, deterioration, and
  adherence detectors from running for patients enrolled fewer than 21 days.
  The gap detector shall still run.

analysis pipeline. This included:

- PostgreSQL schema design (12 tables, migrations, indexes)

  adherence-BP correlation, deterioration detector

- Basic clinician dashboard patient list view
- Initial synthetic reading generation for demo patient 1091
- Shadow mode evaluation script (offline batch validation)

running. Basic dashboard displaying patient list with alerts.


Sprint 2 - March 2026 to May 2026

The second sprint completed the full system and refined everything to demo
quality. Key work included:

- Layer 3 LLM narrative generation with 11 validation checks
- Drug interaction detector (4 deterministic rules, comorbidity escalation)
- Risk tier auto-overrides and nightly reclassification with hysteresis
- Clinician tier override endpoint
- Full briefing page UI (drug interactions, visit agenda, AI summary,
  adherence signal, active problems)
- Alert inbox with escalation and calibration suppression
- Patient PWA (CuffLink) - BP submission, medication confirmation, .ics calendar
- Ask ARIA clinical chatbot with three-layer guardrails
- Full frontend redesign (medical blue system, sparklines, search, pagination)
- Variability detector
- Synthetic data engine full care timeline (1,800+ readings for patient 1091)
- All 4 demo patients seeded and validated via setup_demo.py
- BP Trend single source of truth (trend_avg_systolic field)
- Active briefing filter (excludes past appointment briefings)
- 601 unit tests, ruff check clean

Deliverable: Complete system with all four demo patients, all five Layer 1
detectors, drug interaction detection, patient PWA, clinical chatbot, and
shadow mode achieving 78.4% agreement with physician clinical judgments.


Final Status

The project is complete for prototype purposes. Shadow mode achieved 78.4%
agreement (37 labeled evaluation points, 6 false negatives, 2 false positives)
against the physician-recorded HTN concern assessments from the iEMR dataset.
The target was 80%; the current result is 1.6 percentage points below that
threshold. All false negatives are clinically explainable (cold start,
active treatment response, same-day medication changes). No false negatives
are caused by system bugs.

Layer 3 currently uses OpenAI gpt-4o-mini as a temporary substitute for
Anthropic claude-sonnet-4-20250514 in the demo deployment. The validation
layer operates identically regardless of model. Reverting to the specification
model is a one-line change.

The setup_demo.py script produces ALL CHECKS PASSED for all four demo patients
in a single run.
